About This Map

This web map displays federal camping areas in the United States. Click (or tap) on the map to see those within 30 miles your click location.

Quick Tutorial

The map starts by asking for permission to get your current location and then automatically finds federal camping areas within a 30 mile radius (i.e. - as the crow flies rather than along roads). You can also click or tap on the map to find them. Some places have no federal camping areas within 30 miles. Click on an individual campground marker to display its name. Click the name to be taken to Recreation.gov for lots of information about a particular camping area. You can also change the underlying basemap via the icon in the top right.

The map searches for recreation areas or facilities on federal public lands that have camping. Examples of federal public lands include the National Park Service, U.S. Forest Service, Bureau of Land Management, U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service, etc. It does not include places such as state parks or forests. Some results from the database are general, such as when an entire U.S. Forest Ranger District or National Park gets a point on the map instead of a specific campground within it. Note too that not all camping areas are for overnight use; some are day use only. Please visit the web page for specific information about each location.

Geek Stuff

I like to code and camp so I made the web map for fun. The data is drawn from the USDA's Recreation Information Database (RIDB) API. Programmers and developers, check it out if you are interested in finding out more about what is going on behind the scenes. You can also visit the Leaflet web site for info about this great open source web mapping resource.

More could be done to expand and improve the web map. I may or may not add to it in the future. Please remember it was done for fun rather than to build a fully featured app. Feel free to grab the code and build on it yourself!
